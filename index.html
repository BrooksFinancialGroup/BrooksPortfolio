Overview of Investment Strategies

Tactical

Tactical Asset Allocation is a strategy that involves actively managing and making adjustments to a portfolioâ€™s asset basket, based on market conditions and economic trends, aiming to capitalize on shorter-term opportunities and generate income.

Strategic

Strategic Asset Allocation is a strategy that involves setting target allocations for different securities and periodically/passively manage and rebalance the portfolio to maintain those targets. The strategy is used to achieve long-term investment goals by managing risk and return based on an investors specific risk tolerance and goals.

Core/Satellite

Core/Satellite Asset Allocation is a strategy that involves balancing the stability of SAA and the growth potential of TAA by combining a broadly diversified core portfolio with smaller actively managed satellite positions aimed to generate high yield and profitability.

FCMS Portfolio Translation

Freedom

Freedom, unlike Liberty, houses multiple strategies, and occasionally incorporates both into a single portfolio. This is beneficial for both the relationship and the advisor as it allows an array of options catering to the specific desires and risk of the relationship.

Liberty

Liberty is an FCMS sleeve that, in its entirety, is a core/satellite strategy model. It is composed of the following series. It is composed of an accumulation series, growth and income series with a risk mitigation subsector, and also a multi asset income series.

Freedom Portfolios
Management Risk Series
Purpose: Risk mitigation seeking long term growth with enhanced downside protection.
  1. Low Volatility Equity: Provide exposure to stocks that have historically had low volatility characteristics.
  2. Hedged Equity: Provide exposure to growth potential of the stock market while employing an options strategy designed to reduce downside risk.
  3. Long/Short Equity: Participate in up markets and mitigate risk in down markets by making long and short investments in individual stocks.
  4. Tactical Allocation: Pursues a growth-focused but balanced approach to investing in equity, fixed income, and cash based around a loss avoidance framework.
Protected Growth Series
Purpose: Provide exposure to the growth potential of equity markets while reducing downside risk using buffer ETFs and capped upside potential.
  1. US Protected Growth: Protect against first 10% of losses.
  2. US Protected Growth Moderate: Protect against 15% of losses.
  3. US Protected Growth Conservative: Protect against losses between -5% to -30%.
  4. Global Protected Growth Moderate: Protects against first 15% of losses (diversified instead of basing off S&P 500).
Tactical Allocation Series
Purpose: Tactical Strategies managed through an objective, evidence based research process to reduce emotional bias.
  1. Tactical US Sector Equity ETF: Weight-of-the-evidence approach seeking to enhance risk-adjusted returns relative to the S&P 500 by over and under-weighting sectors.
  2. Tactical US Sector Equity ETF with Stop Loss: Same as above but employs a stop loss model that allocates 50% to cash during periods of heightened market risk.
  3. Tactical Allocation ETF: Growth focused but balanced approach to investing based on a loss avoidance framework (ex: 60% MSCI All Country World Index, 40% Barclays Global Aggregate Bond Index).
  4. Tactical Fixed Income ETF: Combines macroeconomic and technical indicators in a weight-of-the-evidence approach to allocate across nine fixed income sectors and geographies.
Global Allocation Series
Purpose: Capitalize on short-term opportunities.
  1. Global Focused ETF: Maximum long-term growth and appreciation potential (Target Asset Allocation: Equity- 100%, Fixed Income- 0%).
  2. Global Growth ETF: Long-term growth and appreciation potential (Target AA: Equity- 85%, Fixed Income- 15%).
  3. Global Moderate ETF: Moderate long-term growth potential and current income (Target AA: Equity- 65%, Fixed Income- 35%).
  4. Global Conservative ETF: Current income and modest long-term growth potential (Target AA: Equity- 40%, Fixed Income- 60%).
Institutional Equity Series
Purpose: Individual stock based strategies seeking to add value through in depth research and security selection.
  1. US Core Dividend: Total return strategy focused on large capitalization, high-quality companies that pay attractive dividends, and have the ability to grow their dividends.
  2. US Growth Equity: Focused on leadership companies with sector tailwinds to support multi-year investment opportunities where the market price underestimates the magnitude of future growth potential.
  3. US Multi Cap Equity: An all-capitalization strategy seeking to benefit from the long-term compounding of earnings and cash flow through investments in select companies that can be held for long time periods.
  4. Global Value Dividend: A total return strategy focused on global franchise companies that are market leaders in their industry, have historically consistent dividends, and trade at attractive valuations.
  5. International Growth Equity: Invests in companies with compelling long-term growth characteristics that are trading at a discount to intrinsic value.
Freedom Alternative
Purpose: Alternative strategies designed to diversify traditional stock and bond portfolios.
Absolute Return Strategy
Diversified exposure to alternative investment strategies that have historically been less correlated with stock and bond markets (hedged equity, event-driven/arbitrage, Global Macro, Opportunistic Credit).
Risk profile comparable to fixed income, consider using as a diversifier within a conservative allocation.
Real Assets Strategy
Seeks long-term, inflation adjusted appreciation potential (Real estate, Global infrastructure, Natural resources).
Risk profile comparable to equities, consider as a diversifier within a growth allocation.

Risk Evaluation

  • Strategic Risks: Fixed allocation can lead to challenges during market downturns.
  • Tactical Risks: Requires attentive management, may incur higher costs.
  • Core/Satellite Risks: Needs balance and continuous oversight to ensure performance.

How is the score calculated?

Our comprehensive analysis has led to several key recommendations tailored to different investment strategies. These insights are designed to guide investors in aligning their portfolio strategies with their individual financial goals and risk appetites:

  • Strategic Allocation: Ideal for stability and capital preservation, suited for long-term horizons with low risk tolerance. Regular reviews are essential.
  • Tactical Allocation: Recommended for those comfortable with volatility and proactive management. Optimal for flexible allocations based on market shifts.
  • Core/Satellite Strategy: Balances risk and return, combining stable core investments with growth-focused satellite opportunities. Requires diligent monitoring.

Ultimately, investors should choose a strategy based on their unique risk tolerance, investment objectives, and time horizon. Blending different strategies can offer diversified benefits, harnessing the strengths of each approach to achieve comprehensive financial goals.
