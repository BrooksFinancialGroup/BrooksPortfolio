Executive Summary

Mr. Chris DePietro tasked us with researching tactical, strategic, and core/satellite investment strategies. We conducted an in-depth review of historical performance, risk characteristics, and practical applications tailored to FCMS by consulting the FCMS SharePoint platform and Mr. Brian Zabora. Evaluating these strategies, we looked at their benefits and drawbacks concerning volatility, economic shifts, and investment behavior, factoring in fees, turnover, and taxes.

Overview of Investment Strategies

Tactical

Tactical Asset Allocation involves actively managing and adjusting a portfolioâ€™s asset mix based on market conditions and economic trends, aiming to capitalize on short-term opportunities and generate income.

Strategic

Strategic Asset Allocation involves setting target allocations for different securities and passively managing and rebalancing the portfolio to maintain those targets. This strategy aims for long-term goals by managing risk based on specific investor tolerance and objectives.

Core/Satellite

Core/Satellite Asset Allocation combines the stability of a broadly diversified core portfolio (SAA) with the growth potential of actively managed satellite positions (TAA) aimed at generating high yield and profitability.

Risk Evaluation

Strategic Risks
  • Fixed allocation exposes to downturns and underperformance.
  • Potential missed tactical opportunities; allocation drift risk.
  • Requires behavioral discipline; potential liquidity issues in inflationary environments.
Tactical Risks
  • Allows risk mitigation but may lead to emotional bias.
  • Frequent trading increases costs and taxes.
  • Risk of poor timing, leading to underperformance.
Core/Satellite Risks
  • Market risk affects both core and satellite.
  • Over-allocation to satellites may increase risk.
  • Complex management requires ongoing oversight

Recommendations & Final Thoughts

  • Strategic: Suitable for stable growth and risk-averse investors.
  • Tactical: Fits active managers seeking higher returns with oversight.
  • Core/Satellite: Ideal for those balanced between safety and growth; diligent monitoring needed.
  • Blend strategies based on goals and risk tolerance for diversified outcomes.
