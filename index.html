Executive Summary

Mr. Chris DePietro tasked us with researching tactical, strategic, and core/satellite investment strategies. We have conducted an in-depth review of historical performance, risk characteristics, and practical applications that further differentiate the strategies specific to FCMS by reviewing the FCMS SharePoint platform and consulting with Mr. Brian Zabora. We also evaluated the benefits and drawbacks regarding volatility, economic shifts, and investment behavior considering fees, turnover, and taxes.

Overview of Investment Strategies

Tactical

Tactical Asset Allocation involves actively managing and adjusting a portfolio’s asset mix based on market conditions and economic trends. The goal is to capitalize on short-term opportunities and generate higher income.

Strategic

Strategic Asset Allocation sets long-term target allocations for different securities and rebalances passively. It seeks to achieve long-term goals by managing risk/return to match an investor’s tolerance and objectives.

Core/Satellite

Core/Satellite Asset Allocation combines a broadly diversified core portfolio (stability) with smaller, actively managed satellite positions (growth/high yield).

FCMS Portfolio Translation

Freedom
  • Freedom houses multiple strategies, and occasionally incorporates both into a single portfolio for flexible and customized risk/return alignment.
Foundation
  • Long-term, strategic allocation using equities and fixed income.
  • Core Series: Active funds for risk-adjusted returns based on market efficiency.
  • Core ETF Series: Efficient, cost-effective exposure to markets via low-cost, passive ETFs.
  • Tax-Aware Series: Tax-aware rebalancing, allocations to tax-friendly investments and municipal bonds.
  • Sustainable Investing Series: ESG-oriented funds for responsible growth.
Dynamic
  • Active strategies for value-add through TAA, stock selection, and risk mitigation.
    • Managed Risk Series: Low volatility, hedged, long/short, and tactical allocation strategies.
    • Protected Growth Series: Growth with downside protection via buffer ETFs.
    • Tactical Allocation Series: Evidence-based sector weighting, stop-loss, and fixed-income ETF strategies.
    • Global Allocation Series: Short-term global opportunities via diversified asset mixes.
    • Institutional Equity Series: Deep stock selection, focusing on high-quality, dividend, and value-oriented equities.
Alternative
  • Absolute Return: Alternative strategies (hedged equity, global macro, etc.) for diversification, especially in conservative allocations.
  • Real Assets: Real estate, infrastructure, and resources for inflation-adjusted growth.
Liberty
  • Pure Core/Satellite model: accumulation, growth & income, risk mitigation, and multi-asset income series, each with aggressive to conservative options.
  • Liberty is an FCMS sleeve that, in its entirety, is a core/satellite strategy model. It is composed of the following series. It is composed of an accumulation series, growth and income series with a risk mitigation subsector, and also a multi asset income series.

Historical Performance Analysis

Freedom Strategies Evaluation
  • Foundation Series: Steady growth with risk mitigation. Core Series (6% avg), ETF Series (5% avg), Tax-Aware (4.5% avg), Sustainable (6% avg).
  • Dynamic Series:
    • Managed Risk: 6.36% avg, low to moderate variance.
    • Tactical Allocation: 7.4% avg, high variance (up to 12%).
    • Protected Growth: 4.5% avg, low variance, downside protection.
    • Global Allocation: 4.6%-6.9% avg, international flexibility.
    • Institutional Equity: 10% avg, 4% variance, stock selection focus.

Liberty Strategies Evaluation

  • Accumulation Series: High risk/reward, up to 20% in best years, -16% in worst; targets young/high-risk investors.
  • Growth & Income Series: Middle ground for risk/yield; best ~15%, worst ~-15%.
  • Multi-Asset Income Series: Focus on bonds, lower yields (~7-10%), best for older/low-risk investors, strong downside protection.

Risk Evaluation

Strategic
  • Fixed allocation: fully exposed to downturns and underperformance, especially in changing markets.
  • Missed tactical opportunities, possible allocation drift, behavioral discipline required.
  • May lack liquidity in inflationary environments.
Tactical
  • Allows for risk mitigation and exploitation of short-term trends.
  • Frequent trading can increase costs/taxes; requires expertise and may suffer from emotional bias.
  • Market timing risk can lead to underperformance.
Core/Satellite
  • Blends stability and growth; both core and satellite are exposed to market risk.
  • Over-allocation to satellites increases risk; requires ongoing oversight, rebalancing, and research.
  • Complex to manage without strong infrastructure and support.

Recommendations & Final Thoughts

  • Strategic: Best for stable, long-term growth and risk-averse investors.
  • Tactical: Suits active investors/managers seeking higher returns and willing to accept volatility and oversight.
  • Core/Satellite: Ideal for those wanting a blend of safety and growth; requires diligence to avoid overexposure.
  • Choose strategy based on investor goals and risk tolerance. Blend approaches for diversified results.
