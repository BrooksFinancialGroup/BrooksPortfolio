Overview of Investment Strategies

Tactical

Tactical Asset Allocation is a strategy that involves actively managing and making adjustments to a portfolio’s asset basket, based on market conditions and economic trends, aiming to capitalize on shorter-term opportunities and generate income.

Strategic

Strategic Asset Allocation is a strategy that involves setting target allocations for different securities and periodically/passively manage and rebalance the portfolio to maintain those targets. The strategy is used to achieve long-term investment goals by managing risk and return based on an investors specific risk tolerance and goals.

Core/Satellite

Core/Satellite Asset Allocation is a strategy that involves balancing the stability of SAA and the growth potential of TAA by combining a broadly diversified core portfolio with smaller actively managed satellite positions aimed to generate high yield and profitability.

Risk Evaluation

  • Strategic Risks: Fixed allocation can lead to challenges during market downturns.
  • Tactical Risks: Requires attentive management, may incur higher costs.
  • Core/Satellite Risks: Needs balance and continuous oversight to ensure performance.

About the Success Score

  • Success Score = 5-Year Return ÷ 5-Year Standard Deviation (Volatility)
  • This ratio helps investors compare different portfolios by showing how much return they get for each unit of risk.
  • Higher scores mean greater efficiency: more return for the same risk, or less risk for the same return.
  • Mixing composite strategies and benchmarks provides context for evaluating real-world investment choices.

Our comprehensive analysis has led to several key recommendations tailored to different investment strategies. These insights are designed to guide investors in aligning their portfolio strategies with their individual financial goals and risk appetites:

  • Strategic Allocation: Ideal for stability and capital preservation, suited for long-term horizons with low risk tolerance. Regular reviews are essential.
  • Tactical Allocation: Recommended for those comfortable with volatility and proactive management. Optimal for flexible allocations based on market shifts.
  • Core/Satellite Strategy: Balances risk and return, combining stable core investments with growth-focused satellite opportunities. Requires diligent monitoring.

Ultimately, investors should choose a strategy based on their unique risk tolerance, investment objectives, and time horizon. Blending different strategies can offer diversified benefits, harnessing the strengths of each approach to achieve comprehensive financial goals.
