Overview of Investment Strategies

Tactical

Tactical Asset Allocation is a strategy that involves actively managing and making adjustments to a portfolio’s asset basket, based on market conditions and economic trends, aiming to capitalize on shorter-term opportunities and generate income.

Strategic

Strategic Asset Allocation is a strategy that involves setting target allocations for different securities and periodically/passively manage and rebalance the portfolio to maintain those targets. The strategy is used to achieve long-term investment goals by managing risk and return based on an investors specific risk tolerance and goals.

Core/Satellite

Core/Satellite Asset Allocation is a strategy that involves balancing the stability of SAA and the growth potential of TAA by combining a broadly diversified core portfolio with smaller actively managed satellite positions aimed to generate high yield and profitability.

FCMS Portfolio Translation

Freedom

Freedom, unlike Liberty, houses multiple strategies, and occasionally incorporates both into a single portfolio. This is beneficial for both the relationship and the advisor as it allows an array of options catering to the specific desires and risk of the relationship.

Foundation

Freedom Foundation is a series that is long term, strategical asset allocation, traditional approach to portfolio construction using equities and fixed income.

  • Core Series: Deliver improper risk-adjusted returns through active funds based on the degree of market efficiency in each asset class.
  • Core ETF Series: Provide efficient, cost effective exposure to markets by allocating to ETFs that are low cost and passively managed.
  • Tax-Aware Series: Deliver improved tax-adjusted outcomes through a tax-aware rebalancing process and allocations to tax-friendly investments (ETFs and Municipal Bond Funds).
  • Sustainable Investing Series: Incorporates sustainability principles into investment selection process through allocating funds that consider environmental, social, and corporate governance factors.

Risk Evaluation

Risk Evaluation: Strategic, Tactical, and Core-Satellite Approaches

Strategic Asset Allocation (SAA)

Overview: SAA is a long-term investment strategy that maintains a fixed asset allocation, regardless of market conditions.

  • Fixed Exposure: Portfolios remain invested in predetermined asset classes, potentially underperforming during market downturns.
  • Missed Opportunities: Inflexibility may result in missed opportunities in emerging sectors or asset classes.
  • Allocation Drift: Over time, asset allocations may deviate from targets due to market movements, requiring manual rebalancing.
  • Behavioral Risk: Investors may abandon the strategy during market volatility, undermining long-term objectives.
  • Liquidity Constraints: Difficulty in integrating assets that hedge against inflation or provide liquidity during crises.

Tactical Asset Allocation (TAA)

Overview: TAA involves actively adjusting asset allocations based on short-term market forecasts and economic conditions.

  • Transaction Costs: Frequent adjustments can lead to higher transaction fees and tax implications.
  • Market Timing Risk: Incorrect predictions can result in significant losses, especially in volatile markets.
  • Emotional Bias: Active management may be influenced by short-term market sentiments, leading to suboptimal decisions.
  • Complex Management: Requires continuous research and monitoring, increasing the complexity of portfolio management.

Core-Satellite Strategy (C/S)

Overview: Combines a stable, passive core portfolio with actively managed satellite positions to enhance returns.

  • Balance Challenge: Overweighting satellite positions can increase portfolio volatility and risk.
  • Investment Selection Risk: Poor selection of satellite investments can negatively impact overall portfolio performance.
  • Operational Burden: Requires frequent monitoring and rebalancing, demanding significant time and resources.
  • Tool Limitations: Risk assessment tools may not fully capture the nuanced risks associated with satellite investments.
  • Support Advantage: With the backing of FCMS, Brooks Financial Group can effectively manage the complexities of the C/S strategy.

Conclusion

Recent economic developments underscore the importance of adapting investment strategies to navigate the evolving market landscape effectively.

Recommendations & Final Thoughts

Our comprehensive analysis has led to several key recommendations tailored to different investment strategies. These insights are designed to guide investors in aligning their portfolio strategies with their individual financial goals and risk appetites:

  • Strategic Allocation: This approach is ideal for investors prioritizing stability and capital preservation. It suits those with a long-term horizon and a low appetite for risk. By maintaining fixed allocations, strategic allocation minimizes the emotional and market timing risks. However, it may underperform in volatile markets. Regular reviews are essential to ensure the strategy remains aligned with life changes and financial goals.
  • Tactical Allocation: Recommended for investors who are willing to actively manage their portfolios to seize short-term opportunities. This approach is optimal for those comfortable with volatility and a higher level of engagement in market analysis. Tactical allocation can potentially lead to higher returns by permitting flexibility to adjust allocations based on market conditions. However, it demands significant time commitment and may result in higher transaction costs.
  • Core/Satellite Strategy: This balanced approach is excellent for investors looking to combine the safety of a core portfolio with the growth potential of satellite investments. It involves a core focused on broad, stable investments, while satellites target specific opportunities for additional gains. Suitable for those seeking a balance between risk and return, the strategy requires diligent monitoring to ensure satellites do not overly dominate the portfolio.

Ultimately, investors should choose a strategy based on their unique risk tolerance, investment objectives, and time horizon. Blending different strategies can offer diversified benefits, harnessing the strengths of each approach to achieve comprehensive financial goals.
