Executive Summary

Mr. Chris DePietro tasked us with researching tactical, strategic, and core/satellite investment strategies. We conducted an in-depth review of historical performance, risk characteristics, and practical applications tailored to FCMS by consulting the FCMS SharePoint platform and Mr. Brian Zabora. Evaluating these strategies, we looked at their benefits and drawbacks concerning volatility, economic shifts, and investment behavior, factoring in fees, turnover, and taxes.

Overview of Investment Strategies

  • Tactical Asset Allocation: Involves actively managing portfolio components to benefit from shorter-term opportunities and respond to economic changes.
  • Strategic Asset Allocation: Focuses on long-term target allocations, periodically rebalancing to maintain these targets, catering to specific risk tolerance and goals.
  • Core/Satellite Asset Allocation: Combines a stable core with actively managed satellite positions to generate growth and yield.

Risk Evaluation

The evaluation of risk associated with strategic, tactical, and core/satellite allocations highlights different profiles and consideration points. Understanding these is essential for aligning investment goals with risk appetite.

  • Strategic Allocations: Fixed allocation may lead to underperformance due to market changes.
  • Tactical Allocations: Requires active management, implying more costs and potential missteps in timing.
  • Core/Satellite Allocations: Balance is critical to optimize returns and manage risks.

Recommendations & Final Thoughts

  • Strategic: Best suited for stable growth and risk-averse investors.
  • Tactical: Ideal for those seeking aggressive growth managed with oversight.
  • Core/Satellite: Effective for balancing growth and stability through active rebalancing.
  • Overall Advice: Choose strategies aligning with specific goals and risk thresholds for diversified outcomes.
