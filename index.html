Overview of Investment Strategies

Tactical

Tactical Asset Allocation is a strategy involving active management to adjust a portfolio's asset basket, capitalize on short-term opportunities, and generate income based on market conditions and economic trends.

Strategic

Strategic Asset Allocation involves setting target allocations for securities and periodically/passively rebalancing the portfolio to maintain those targets, focusing on long-term goals by managing risk and return based on specific investor tolerance.

Core/Satellite

Core/Satellite Asset Allocation balances stability and growth potential by combining a diversified core portfolio with actively managed satellite positions aimed at generating high yield and profitability.

Risk Evaluation: Understanding Asset Allocation Strategies

Strategic Asset Allocation (SAA)

Overview: A long-term, passive investment approach that maintains a fixed asset mix regardless of market conditions.

  • Fixed Exposure: Portfolios stay invested in underperforming assets during downturns.
  • Missed Opportunities: Inflexible strategy fails to respond to short-term market trends.
  • Allocation Drift: Asset weightings shift unintentionally over time without rebalancing.
  • Behavioral Risk: Investors may abandon strategy during downturns due to emotional stress.
  • Liquidity Constraints: Difficulty integrating inflation-hedging or real-asset classes.

Tactical Asset Allocation (TAA)

Overview: An active strategy that adjusts allocations based on short-term market forecasts and opportunities.

  • Transaction Costs: Frequent trading leads to fees and possible tax impacts.
  • Market Timing Risk: Predicting short-term movements can backfire.
  • Emotional Bias: Active management is vulnerable to poor, emotion-driven decisions.
  • Complex Management: Requires continuous research and oversight.

Core-Satellite Strategy (C/S)

Overview: Combines a passive “core” with actively managed “satellite” investments aimed at generating excess return.

  • Balance Challenge: Overweighting satellites can increase volatility.
  • Investment Selection Risk: Selecting poor-performing active components skews portfolio performance.
  • Operational Burden: Requires frequent monitoring, rebalancing, and resources.
  • Tool Limitations: Tools like Nitrogen may not fully capture nuanced risk profiles.
  • Support Advantage: With FCMS support, Brooks Financial Group can manage the strategy effectively.
