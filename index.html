Executive Summary

We evaluated Strategic, Tactical, and Core/Satellite investment strategies using FCMS data and insights. This site serves as a knowledge hub for onboarding new members into Brooks Financial Group’s approach to portfolio construction and performance evaluation.

Investment Strategy Overview

Strategic

Buy-and-hold allocation model with fixed targets. Simple and effective long-term but lacks adaptability during market downturns.

Tactical

Flexible, actively-managed model aiming to exploit short-term market conditions. Offers high upside but increased complexity and cost.

Core/Satellite

Balanced approach blending Strategic and Tactical. A passive “core” anchors the portfolio while “satellites” chase growth opportunities.

Risk Evaluations

Strategic Risks

  • Limited responsiveness during downturns
  • Behavioral drag (panic-selling in drawdowns)
  • Allocation drift over time

Tactical Risks

  • Requires expert management and market timing
  • Higher transaction and tax costs
  • Emotional and decision fatigue

Core/Satellite Risks

  • Overexposure to volatile satellite positions
  • Operational demands (monitoring and rebalancing)
  • Misalignment of risk profile if poorly executed

Recommendations

  • Use Core/Satellite as the default framework with FCMS support
  • Deploy Tactical strategies within satellites to capture short-term gains
  • Apply Strategic allocation for conservative clients prioritizing long-term capital preservation
