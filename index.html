Executive Summary

Mr. Chris DePietro tasked us with researching tactical, strategic, and core/satellite investment strategies. We have conducted an in-depth review of historical performance, risk characteristics, and practical applications that further differentiate the strategies specific to FCMS by reviewing the FCMS SharePoint platform and consulting with Mr. Brian Zabora. We also evaluated the benefits and drawbacks regarding volatility, economic shifts, and investment behavior considering fees, turnover, and taxes.

Overview of Investment Strategies

Tactical

Tactical Asset Allocation is a strategy involving active management to adjust a portfolio's asset basket, capitalize on short-term opportunities, and generate income based on market conditions and economic trends.

Strategic

Strategic Asset Allocation involves setting target allocations for securities and periodically/passively rebalancing the portfolio to maintain those targets, focusing on long-term goals by managing risk and return based on specific investor tolerance.

Core/Satellite

Core/Satellite Asset Allocation balances stability and growth potential by combining a diversified core portfolio with actively managed satellite positions aimed at generating high yield and profitability.

FCMS Portfolio Translation

Freedom Portfolio

Freedom, unlike Liberty, houses multiple strategies and incorporates both into a single portfolio. This flexibility benefits both the relationship and the advisor by catering to specific desires and risks.

Foundation Series
  • Core Series: Delivers risk-adjusted returns through active funds tailored to the degree of market efficiency in each asset class.
  • Core ETF Series: Provides cost-effective market exposure by allocating to low-cost, passively managed ETFs.
  • Tax-Aware Series: Utilizes tax-aware rebalancing, focusing on investments like ETFs and municipal bonds for improved tax-adjusted outcomes.
  • Sustainable Investing Series: Incorporates ESG principles, investing in funds that consider environmental, social, and corporate governance factors.
Dynamic

Active strategies in the Dynamic series add value through TAA, stock selection, and risk mitigation, subdivided into:

  • Managed Risk Series: Incorporates Low Volatility Equity, Hedged Equity, Long/Short Equity, and Tactical Allocation.
  • Protected Growth Series: Uses buffer ETFs for capital preservation with capped upside.
  • Tactical Allocation Series: Employs objective, evidence-based processes to reduce emotional bias.
  • Global Allocation Series: Focuses on short-term global opportunities.
  • Institutional Equity Series: In-depth stock selection aimed at adding value through research.
  • Alternative Strategies: Offers diversified alternative investments less correlated with traditional markets, such as Absolute Return and Real Assets strategies.

Historical Performance Analysis

Foundation Series

The Foundation Series combines passive and active management to deliver steady growth with risk mitigation across various asset allocations:

  • Core Series: Moderate average returns of about 6% over five years. Offers a stable performance structure favoring capital preservation.
  • Core ETF Series: Focuses on cost-effectiveness with a 5% average over five years, appealing to those favoring managed ETFs.
  • Tax-Aware Series: Maintains consistent performance with a focus on tax efficiency, suitable for low-variance seekers.
  • Sustainable Series: Achieves about 6% over five years with a focus on ethical values and sustainability practices.

Freedom Strategies Evaluation

Freedom Strategies feature a combination of risk mitigation, tactical allocation, and growth-oriented approaches:

  • Managed Risk: Averages a 6.36% return, focusing on risk mitigation for conservative investors.
  • Tactical Allocation: Offers a 7.4% return with higher risks but potential rewards, engaging investors in market dynamics.
  • Protected Growth: Balances risk-reduction with equity market engagement, yielding 4.5% over five years.
  • Global Allocation: Adapts to global opportunities with returns reduced over recent years, appealing to speculative investors.
  • Institutional Equity: Achieves a strong return of 10%, emphasizing thorough stock research.

Liberty Strategies Evaluation

The Liberty Strategies consist of accumulation, growth, and income, incorporating varying risk levels:

  • Accumulation Series: High volatility and high reward, suited for risk-tolerant individuals over long horizons.
  • Growth & Income Series: Balances growth with income, targeting those with some risk tolerance.
  • Multi-Asset Income Series: Emphasizes stability over high yields, favoring older or low-risk investors.

Risk Evaluation

  • Strategic Risks: Fixed allocation can lead to challenges during market downturns.
  • Tactical Risks: Requires attentive management and may incur higher costs.
  • Core/Satellite Risks: Needs balance and continuous oversight to ensure performance.

Recommendations & Final Thoughts

  • Strategic: Best for stability and long-term growth. Suits risk-averse investors.
  • Tactical: Ideal for those seeking active strategies with higher potential returns and oversight.
  • Core/Satellite: Effective for balancing safety and growth through active management. Requires diligent monitoring.
  • Overall Advice: Align your strategy with specific goals and risk tolerances for diversified results.
